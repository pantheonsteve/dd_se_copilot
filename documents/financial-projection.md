## What Has to Go Right

Path A isn't guaranteed. Here's what has to happen:

1. **The business has to actually launch and grow.** $75k by year 2 is achievable but requires consistent effort — 15–20 hours/week on top of your day job. The agent system you're building is both a competitive advantage AND the foundation of a potential product.

2. **Datadog (or equivalent) has to keep performing.** If the observability market contracts or Datadog stumbles, your W-2 and equity take a hit. Diversification matters.

3. **You have to become known.** LinkedIn posts, conference talks, a newsletter, a course — something that puts your name in front of people. Harvard + Datadog + AI expertise is a powerful brand if you actually deploy it.

4. **Spending discipline.** The $810/month gap has to close permanently. The house move has to be at $1.3M, not $1.6M, unless business income is proven by then.

5. **Health and marriage.** None of this works if you burn out or if the side hustle strains your family. The agent system should *save* you time, not consume it.

If you execute on becoming the go-to AI expert for sales engineering over the next 3–5 years, you're looking at a **$5–7M net worth by age 60**, with both kids through college debt-free, a paid-off home, and the option to work on your own terms.

That's the difference between "I made a good living" and "I built real wealth."

The gap between Path A and Path C is not talent. It's not intelligence. It's execution and positioning. You have the raw materials. The question is whether you build the machine.
